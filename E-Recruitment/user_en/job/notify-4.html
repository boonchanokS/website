Writing skills sufficient to compose and edit a variety of documents using correct spelling and grammar with the ability to pay close attention to detail and proofread work carefully.
  2. Ability to work accurately, with interruptions in a flexible way that allows for changes in assignments and priorities depending upon identified needs of the unit.
  3. Organizational skills sufficient to prioritize work and complete assignments accurately, either independently or as part of a team, under pressure of competing deadlines and with frequent interruptions, working from own initiative and/or following direction, policies, or procedures.

  5. Ability to exercise independence and judgement where required.
  6. Computer skill: experience working in a computerized office environment with word processing, database and spreadsheet skills sufficient to prepare correspondence, reports, forms, statistical/technical/scientific materials, etc. with speed and accuracy, including the willingness to learn and use new computer programs/applications.

  • Qualifications

Job Description

For a manager of the JPSS

  1. Monitor the JPSS performance through the entire end-to-end process of publication
  2. Maintain and update JPSS website. Coordinate with editors for new submissions; produce Turnitin report for editors; receive revised submissions and verify completeness; monitor and follow-up on outstanding submissions with editors; monitor and improve quality of reviewer database; proofread the paper and ensure correct format of each publication
  3. Forward submissions to reviewers; drive and monitor the review process, i.e., ensuring each paper is sent to the appropriate number of reviewers; send reminder and update to editors, authors and reviewers
  4. Preparation and maintenance of annual publication schedules, with attention to budgetary and publishing requirements.
  5. Continuously plan and improve the journal work process
  6. Replan where necessary to ensure on-time publication
  7. Generate performance report and update to the editor-in-chief
  8. Act as a focal person to coordinate and handle all issues for editors, editor-in-chief, reviewers, authors and other stakeholders
  9. Provide information to authors regarding publication process and policy  

For an English instructor

  1. Perform teaching and course development for the “Reading and Writing in the Social Sciences Courses”.
  2. Being an individual writing tutorials and mentorship for the “Reading and Writing in the Social Sciences Courses”.
